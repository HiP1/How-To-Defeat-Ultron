# How to Defeat Ultron Without the Avengers?

**What Are We Actually Building? How Do We Contain It? And the Missing Killswitch**

**Author:** Ivan Phan (Independent Researcher) · ORCID [0009-0003-1095-5855](https://orcid.org/0009-0003-1095-5855)
**DOI:** [10.5281/zenodo.20739166](https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.20739166)
**Licence:** CC BY 4.0 · **Date:** June 2026

---

A critical essay, the epilogue to *The Training Landscape* series.

Everyone in AI safety already knows how the story ends: the system grows capable, its goals diverge, it hides the divergence until hiding no longer matters, and the window for shutting it down has closed. The field's vocabulary carries that ending in its bones, and its apparatus is built to it. The story has a name, and this essay asks its question in earnest: how do you defeat Ultron? In the canon, containment lasted seconds and the rescue took what no laboratory has. A discipline cannot borrow the fictional threat while quietly assuming the fictional rescue comes with it.

So the essay asks the two questions that actually carry the weight: where the field's picture of the ending came from, and how much of it the published record actually carries. It follows the threat model to its origin through an eighty-two-case catalogue of the fiction the field formed its imagination on, then tests it against the behaviours logged from 2024 to 2026 under emergent misalignment, alignment faking, and reward hacking, read at the level of mechanism rather than headline. From there it goes beneath the single agent the field reasons about, to the substrate these systems run on, the scaffold they are deployed inside, and the question of who answers for what they do.

The argument is a falsifiable chain built on primary sources and the measured record. It is written for two readers who seldom share a page: the one who weighs a claim by its evidence, and the one who watches how a technical field talks itself into a worldview. The title's question gets an answer, in the final section. It is shorter than the field will expect, and it needs nothing the field does not already have.
